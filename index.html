Project Context and Creative Roles

Each of the audiovisual works presented in my portfolio serves as a step in my ongoing artistic development — either in collaboration with other directors or through authorial initiatives. These projects span fiction and essay film, combining elements of performance, research, and editing experimentation.

My roles have varied across productions:

  • As a Director: I was responsible for the overall creative vision, guiding the story’s visual and emotional arc from pre-production to post. In Karoshi, for instance, I also co-wrote the screenplay, merging narrative structure with visual rhythm and tone.
  • As an Assistant Director (AD): I worked to bridge the gap between the creative intent of the director and the logistical execution of the set. In both A Caixa and Mozart: o Brasil em 1m², my tasks included managing the shooting schedule, organizing the crew and cast flow, and ensuring that each take aligned with the overall vision and time constraints.
  • As an Editor: Particularly in the essay film Dissídio, I developed a montage rooted in archival imagery and poetic juxtaposition. This work required crafting a rhythm and meaning almost entirely through editing, with no live-action footage.
  • As a Screenwriter: My contribution came in building themes and character arcs that aligned with the director’s vision, focusing on story economy and internal structure.

These experiences not only reflect my technical range but also my ability to collaborate in different roles within independent film environments, especially in Brazilian short-form productions. Each project was an opportunity to refine my sensitivity to pacing, tone, and narrative intention — whether directing, supporting direction, assembling a film in the edit, or constructing its textual foundation.

About Me

I am a Brazilian filmmaker and assistant director. I studied Film Directing at the Academia Internacional de Cinema (Filmworks program) and currently pursue a professional acting course at Escola de Teatro Macunaíma. I also hold degrees in International Relations (PUC-SP, 2012) and Economics (Insper, 2018), which enrich my creative perspective in visual storytelling.
